📐 V-Dipole (Easiest)

Two telescoping elements at 120° angle, mounted horizontally. Point them north-south. Place outdoors or on a windowsill. Best for 137 MHz (NOAA, Meteor).

Each element: 53.4 cm (λ/4 at 137 MHz) · Angle: 120°

🌀 QFH — Quadrifilar Helix (Best for satellites)

Circular polarization captures satellite signals from all angles as they pass overhead. The best antenna for weather satellites. Build from coax cable and PVC pipe. Many online guides.

Height: ~50 cm for 137 MHz · Material: RG58 coax + PVC

📏 Yagi-Uda (Best gain, directional)

High-gain directional antenna. Must be pointed at the satellite as it passes. Best for weak signals: ISS SSTV, amateur satellites, LEO voice contacts. 3-7 elements.

Length: ~1m for 145 MHz · Gain: 8-12 dBi
💡Start with the included V-dipole. Upgrade to QFH when you want better NOAA images. Yagi for ISS contacts.

🌤️ Receive NOAA Weather Images

This is the #1 beginner SDR satellite project. NOAA 18 and 19 broadcast live weather images as they pass overhead. The signal is analog — you record audio and decode it into an image. Each pass gives a ~2000 km wide swath of your region.

📋 Step-by-Step

Find the pass. Use heavens-above.com or the Look4Sat app. NOAA 18/19 pass every ~2 hours. You need passes above 20° elevation. Higher = better signal.
Set up antenna. V-dipole at 120°, elements at 53.4 cm each. Place horizontally outdoors or on a windowsill. Point elements roughly north-south.
Tune your SDR. Open SDR# (Windows) or GQRX (Mac/Linux). Set frequency: 137.1000 MHz for NOAA 19, 137.9125 MHz for NOAA 18. Mode: WFM (Wide FM). Bandwidth: 40 kHz.
Record the pass. When the satellite appears, you'll see a signal on the waterfall. Record the audio as a .wav file. The pass lasts 10-15 minutes. You'll hear a characteristic "tick-tock" warbling sound.
Decode the image. Open the .wav in WXtoImg (free) or SatDump. It converts the audio into a weather image — visible or infrared, depending on time of day.
Admire your image. You just received a live satellite photo directly from space. No internet needed. You literally captured photons reflected off clouds, digitized by a satellite, broadcast as radio waves, and decoded on your computer.
☀️ Sunlight→ 🌍 Earth→ 🛰️ NOAA sensor→ 📡 137 MHz signal→ 📻 Your SDR→ 🔊 Audio .wav→ 🖼️ Weather image
⚡Pro tip: Record in stereo. One channel = audio, other channel = Doppler correction. SatDump handles this automatically. Your first image WILL be noisy — that's normal. Better antenna = better images.

🏗️ Listen to the ISS

The ISS has an amateur radio on 145.800 MHz. Sometimes astronauts make live voice contacts with schools and ham operators. The station also transmits SSTV (Slow Scan TV) images during special events — actual photos from space!

📋 How to Listen

Find ISS pass. Use ISS Detector app or heavens-above.com. You need a pass above 30° for decent reception. Pass lasts ~6-10 minutes.
Tune to 145.800 MHz. Mode: NFM (Narrow FM). Bandwidth: 12 kHz. The signal will Doppler-shift ±3.5 kHz during the pass — adjust manually or use Doppler correction.
Listen. If astronauts are transmitting, you'll hear voice. The APRS beacon sends digital packets (sounds like "brrzt"). During SSTV events, you hear warbling tones — decode with MMSSTV or phone apps like Robot36.
📅SSTV events happen a few times per year (check ARISS.org). During events, the ISS continuously transmits images for days. Even a simple V-dipole can receive them. Point your phone's mic at the speaker and Robot36 app decodes it in real time!

✈️ Track Aircraft with ADS-B

Every commercial aircraft broadcasts its position, altitude, speed, and callsign on 1090 MHz. Your RTL-SDR can receive these signals and plot aircraft on a live map — like FlightRadar24, but running locally from your own antenna.

📋 Quick Setup

Install dump1090. Free software: dump1090 (Windows/Linux) or tar1090 (Raspberry Pi). One command to start.
Use the included antenna at full extension, or build a simple 1/4-wave ground plane (6.9 cm element + 4 ground radials).
Launch and watch. Open localhost:8080 in your browser. Aircraft appear on a map with live positions. Range: 100-400 km depending on antenna height and line of sight.

This is the easiest SDR project. Literally plug in, run one program, see aircraft. Works indoors if near a window.

🔧 Software Guide

All software is free and open source.

📻 SDR Receivers

SDR# (Windows) — Most popular. Clean interface. Plugin ecosystem. Download from airspy.com.

SDR++ (All platforms) — Modern, fast, cross-platform. Excellent waterfall. Recommended for Mac/Linux.

GQRX (Mac/Linux) — Mature, reliable. Gnuradio-based.

CubicSDR (All) — Simple, beginner-friendly.

🛰️ Satellite Decoders

SatDump — All-in-one decoder for NOAA, Meteor, GOES, and more. Active development. Recommended.

WXtoImg — Classic NOAA APT decoder. No longer developed but still works.

MeteorDemod — Specialized Meteor-M2 LRPT decoder.

✈️ Aircraft Tracking

dump1090 — The original ADS-B decoder. Run from command line.

tar1090 — Beautiful web interface for dump1090. Raspberry Pi friendly.

FlightAware PiAware — Complete Raspberry Pi image. Plug and play.

📅 Pass Prediction

Look4Sat (Android) — Best mobile pass predictor. Free.

Heavens-Above (web) — Classic. Reliable. Shows exact pass paths.

Gpredict (desktop) — Full satellite tracking with Doppler control.

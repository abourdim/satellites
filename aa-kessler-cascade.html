The Kessler Cascade

In 1978, NASA scientist Donald Kessler predicted that if enough objects accumulate in orbit, a single collision could trigger a chain reaction — each impact creating debris that causes more impacts, until entire orbital shells become unusable.

There are currently ~36,500 tracked objects in orbit. Millions of smaller fragments are untracked. At orbital speeds (28,000 km/h), a 1cm piece of debris hits with the force of a hand grenade.

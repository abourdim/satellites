You Are Here
Standing on the surface of a planet 4.5 billion years old, 12,742 km across, spinning at 1,670 km/h. You don't feel it.
☽ Heritage
Al-Biruni (973 CE) calculated this planet's radius using a mountain and trigonometry — accurate to within 16 km.

Birds & Skyscrapers
The Eiffel Tower (330m). Most birds fly here. Pigeons, crows, sparrows. The air is thick, warm, and full of life. 99.99% of the atmosphere is still above you.

meters — Low Clouds
Cumulus Clouds
The puffy white clouds you see on a summer day. Temperature has dropped ~13°C since sea level. Small planes fly here. You can still breathe normally.
A single cumulus cloud weighs approximately 500,000 kg — the weight of 100 elephants, suspended in air.

Roof of the World
The highest point humans can stand. Air pressure is one-third of sea level. Without supplemental oxygen, you'd lose consciousness in minutes. The jet stream screams past at 200 km/h.

Commercial Aircraft
FL350-FL390. Temperature outside: -56°C. Air pressure: 25% of sea level. Every passenger jet window is the only thing between you and instant death. The sky above starts turning dark blue.

Upper Stratosphere
The ozone layer protects everything below. Weather balloons reach here, taking measurements that feed the forecasts on your phone. The sky is nearly black. You can see Earth's curvature.
🎈
100
km — The Kármán Line
The Edge of Space
The internationally recognized boundary of space. Air is 0.00003% of sea level pressure. Above this line, aerodynamic flight is impossible — only orbital mechanics work. You are officially an astronaut.
If Earth were a basketball, the entire atmosphere would be thinner than a sheet of paper wrapped around it.
🌌
550
km — Low Earth Orbit
Starlink Satellites
Over 5,000 Starlink satellites orbit here, forming the largest constellation in history. Each weighs 260 kg and moves at 27,000 km/h. They deliver internet to remote corners of the planet.
☽ Heritage
The algorithms predicting where each satellite will be — the word itself comes from al-Khwarizmi (780 CE), father of algebra.
🛰️
408
km — International Space Station
🛰️ The ISS
Six humans live here right now. 109 meters wide — the size of a football pitch. Orbits every 92 minutes, seeing 16 sunrises per day. Moving at 28,000 km/h — fast enough to travel from London to New York in 12 minutes.

547
km — Hubble Space Telescope
The Eye Above the Atmosphere
Above the blurring atmosphere, Hubble has photographed galaxies 13.4 billion light-years away — looking back to when the universe was just 400 million years old.
☽ Heritage
Ibn al-Haytham (965 CE) wrote the Book of Optics, proving how light works. Every lens in Hubble descends from his understanding.
🔭
20,200
km — Medium Earth Orbit
GPS Satellites
31 satellites orbiting at 14,000 km/h. Each carries an atomic clock accurate to 1 nanosecond. Your phone triangulates signals from 4+ of them to find your location within 3 meters. Orbital period: 12 hours.
☽ Heritage
Al-Biruni calculated Earth's radius. Al-Battani refined trigonometry. GPS depends on both — measuring distance from a planet whose size they first determined.
📡
35,786
km — Geostationary Orbit
The Stationary Ring
At exactly this altitude, a satellite orbits at the same speed Earth rotates — appearing to hover motionless over one spot. TV broadcasts, weather satellites, and communications relays live here. Arabsat, Türksat, Nilesat — all at this altitude.
Arthur C. Clarke predicted this orbit in 1945. It's sometimes called the "Clarke Belt" — but the mathematics go back to Kepler and the Islamic astronomers who influenced him.
📺
384,400
km — The Moon
Our Nearest Neighbor
Light takes 1.3 seconds to reach the Moon. 12 humans have walked on it. It's drifting away at 3.8 cm per year. From here, Earth is a pale blue dot — and every satellite, every plane, every person is invisible.

☽ Heritage
Al-Sufi (964 CE) mapped the stars the Moon travels past. Abbas ibn Firnas built a planetarium to simulate the heavens. A thousand years later, we walk on them.

One Last Thought

You just scrolled from 0 to 384,400 km. In reality, if this page were truly to scale, the Moon would be 27 kilometers of scrolling away from the ISS section. The atmosphere section would be invisible — a single pixel.

Space is incomprehensibly vast. The thin blue line of our atmosphere is all that separates us from infinity. And within that sliver, everything we've ever known — every person, every city, every forest — exists.

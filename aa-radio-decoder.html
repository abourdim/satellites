📡 How to Receive

NOAA weather satellites broadcast at 137 MHz. A $20 RTL-SDR dongle + V-dipole antenna + WXtoImg software = live satellite images from your backyard. No license needed to listen.

🔊 What It Sounds Like

NOAA APT sounds like a rhythmic ticking/whooshing — each line of the image encoded as audio tones. ISS voice is normal FM radio. CubeSat beacons are short digital bursts.

📊 Signal Strength

At 137 MHz, NOAA signals arrive at about -100 dBm. GPS signals at Earth's surface: -130 dBm. That's 0.000000000000001 watts. Below thermal noise. Incredible engineering.

☽ Heritage

Receiving satellite signals is modern astronomy. Like al-Sufi cataloging stars by eye, amateur operators catalog satellite passes by ear. The tools changed; the act of watching the sky didn't.

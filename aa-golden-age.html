The Golden Age

From the 8th to 14th century, Arab and Muslim scholars built the mathematical and scientific foundations that made modern satellites possible.

"The ink of the scholar is holier than the blood of the martyr."

A Continuous Thread

Every GPS fix uses al-Khwarizmi's algebra. Every satellite orbit was predicted by al-Tusi's models. Every telescope lens follows Ibn al-Haytham's optics. Every star we navigate by carries al-Sufi's Arabic name. The Golden Age didn't end — its knowledge flows through every satellite above us today.

🔄 Why orbit is "falling"

An orbiting satellite is falling toward Earth constantly — but moving sideways so fast that the ground curves away beneath it. Orbit is perpetual free-fall with enough horizontal speed to keep missing.

⬆️ Speed up = go higher

Counterintuitively, to reach a higher orbit you accelerate in the direction of travel. This raises the opposite side of your orbit. Then you burn again at the top to circularize. This is a Hohmann transfer.

🧮 Delta-V Budget

Δv is the currency of spaceflight. Every maneuver costs delta-v. LEO → GEO costs ~3.9 km/s. LEO → Moon: ~3.1 km/s. LEO → Mars: ~3.6 km/s. Every gram of fuel matters.

☽ Heritage

Al-Tusi (1201 CE) described circular motion through combinations of circles — the "Tusi couple." This mathematical tool for composing orbital motions anticipated the elliptical transfer orbits calculated 700 years later.

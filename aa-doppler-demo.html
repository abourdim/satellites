📐 The Math

f_observed = f_source × (1 + v_radial/c)

Where v_radial is the velocity component toward/away from you, and c = speed of light. The ISS on 145.8 MHz shifts by ±3.5 kHz — easily heard on a ham radio.

📻 Real-World Doppler

Ground stations must constantly retune to track satellite signals. GPS receivers correct for Doppler to maintain lock. The shift tells you the satellite's velocity — used in navigation since the 1960s (Transit system).

🔭 Doppler in Astronomy

The same effect reveals galaxy speeds, exoplanets, and the expanding universe. Blueshifted = approaching. Redshifted = receding. Hubble's discovery of the expanding universe relied on Doppler shift of starlight.

🛰️ Satellite Tracking

During the Cold War, scientists tracked Sputnik by its Doppler shift on 20.005 MHz. This insight led to the Transit navigation system — GPS's ancestor. One signal, one pass, one position fix.

☽ Heritage

Doppler shift is a wave phenomenon. Ibn al-Haytham (965 CE) established that light behaves as waves with measurable properties. His framework for understanding wave propagation — finite speed, straight-line travel, frequency-dependent behavior — is exactly what makes Doppler shift calculable.
